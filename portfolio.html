Personal Blog

– A Jekyll-based personal blog of publishing articles, Oct 2015

This is a personal blog I made myself to publish my articles, hoping to build a platform to share my ideas with other people. In turn, I can receive reader’s feedback to improve myself, which is also a impulse to push me forward. After finishing the user research and drawing very simple sketch, I designed and coded the website simultaneously by HTML/CSS/Javascript.

Features include:

  • Easy, quick deployment: GitHub sever, namespace domain, Jekyll template
  • Simple, nice design: sort by date, category navigation, previous/next page, back to top button, contact button
  • Flexible technique application: Disqus comment plugin, Google analytics, Sass, jQuery, Markdown editting content

The blog, so far after publishing, attracts 745 users in total and the daily sessions about the blog is around 45. More than 10 articles has been forwarded by several media platforms or websites, which received good comments and feedback. Furthermore, the page view of each article is approximately 1000 now.

Personal Blog

This is my undergraduate portfolio site. Because I want to show all projects I have done during my undergraduate years to recruiters, I built the site all by myself, which includes 6 projects in total. Each project has four parts: name, introduction, access (account number & passcode) and link.

Features include:

Meanwhile, the process of design honed my mindset of thinking from user’s viewpoint, such as how to design interesting website that user would like to use.

Static Blog

– A static self-introduction blog, May 2014

This is my first self-designed website. Back then, I just began studying Front-end for 3 months. And because I was studying in Germany, I wanted to write a website to memorize the half year as an exchange student. The site mainly has 5 parts: scene, song, story, fun and about, which records my according interests.

  • Well-organized codes: CCS3, original Process-oriented Javascript coding

The site took me about 2 months in total to finish. Above all, this experience built my confidence that I have ability to design and code stuff through self-studying.

Shopping Site

– A second hand products shopping website, Dec 2015

When I came to University of Kansas, I found many Chinese have the demand to buy second hand products but they lack the channel to find related information. To better help local Chinese, I determined to built information sharing website, through which people can publish products information, search given products, and browse products by category. Therefore, I asked 2 friends to work with together: one is full-stack developer, one is responsible for marketing. And my main job is to manage the team, design the product & interface, improve the usability and maintain relationships with customers.

Features include:

  • Mature, secure deployment: Aliyun server, git code management
  • Front-end based full stack technique: Sass, NodeJS, MongoDB
  • Comprehensive operation mechanism: Wechat official account, QQ zone, Email, off-line advertisement

Our team just published the Beta 1.0. We are planning to develop new functions based on user feedback. So far, we have already 69 loyal customers and the sessions of website is gradually rising up as well. My goal is to have at least 500 loyal customers. By then, I would like transfer the site to a profit site. Now, it is non-profit.

Exchange Site

– A bonus point exchange mobile website, Feb 2015

This is a project that I was wholly responsible for when I interned for user experience designer at YiFang Information Co., Ltd. Customers can use bonus point they acquired to exchange real goods on this website. My main job contains user research, analysis for competitive products, product & interface design and interaction prototype design (by Axure). Meanwhile, I needed to cooperate with my manager, user interface designers, and Front-end & Back-end developers to maximize the benefits of the company. To better make the site into practice, I assisted the operation manager to streamline the exchange process.

Features include:

  • Responsive design: @media attributes
  • Single page application: Sass, AngularJS, connect Back-end by using restfulAPI, PHP
  • Convenient Database management system: PHP, Ecshop

The website was finished well before the deadline, exceeding my manager’s expectation. And it is still used by the company, which runs well.

Convergent Network Interface

– A harmonious future interface visionDec 2015

This is the final project of interaction design course. We four students work as a team trying to understand how people mentally represent themselves and their interests in the world in relation to their use of technology to meet their needs, interests and desires. We Built personas and scenarios based upon data collected from potential users first. Then we organized ideas through wireframes and interactive video made by Sketch & Invison. By the way, we used Mural, Google docs adn Google Hangout to collaborate with each other.

Features include:

  • Biometric authentication: fingerprint, retina, voice
  • Cross-Platform: desktop, laptop, tablet, mobile phone
  • Artificial intelligence: self-adaption, automatic humanized prompt

The final shared document is well-organized and article about the project got 900+ page-views. And our team got A for the course.
