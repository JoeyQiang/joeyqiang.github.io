Joey Qiang

Sofy VR

  • UX design
  • July, 2016
  • SOFY

I participated in the virtual reality project of SOFY as an intern UX designer at Mobile Now Group (MNG). SOFY VR game is a mobile shooter game. Users need to download SOFY app on their smart-phone firstly, and after they launch the game, they need to wear VR glasses to play the game by moving the crosshair on the enemies.

It's an interesting and challenging experience for me because it’s the first time that I had the responsibility to directly negotiate with the client. Not only should I collaborate with my colleagues to finish my design work, but also I had to present our team’s ideas to the client to move the project forward.

Context

We needed to get more done within a short time

MNG worked as an agency to help SOFY design a mobile VR game. Due to SOFY’s urgent schedule, we needed to finish game design in the first week so the VR game developer company, called Ant Hive, could develop the game demo in advance, saving time for testing.

Later, we had to hand over remaining deliverables to them, including wireframes and user interfaces. Therefore, as a interaction designer, I had to push myself and my teammates to work in a rapidly iterated pace for the purpose of meeting the deadline.

After the game were finished, SOFY will advertise the game by binding a free VR glasses to its products, attracting users to buy their products.

Our goal is to design an interesting and easy-play VR shooting game for teen girls with the purpose of increasing the recognition of SOFY.

Game concept

Shooting the foods which are considered bad for girls experiencing menstruation

Because we wanted to relate the game to SOFY’s sanitary pads, finally we determined to design the game’s characters as foods that girls cannot eat when experiencing menstruation. Besides, due to our target audience were girls among 18-24 years old, we set cute cartoon as the game style.

Below were challenges we faced then:

  1. Aiming to shoot is the only interaction mode we can use, while balancing the interesting and complexity of the game.
  2. Collaborating well with the developer company to implement design. while meeting the urgent deadline.

To make the game easy to play, users only need to do one thing — moving the crosshair to the enemy they want to shoot. And there is no time length during the game. Users only focus on shooting enemies as fast as possible since we wanted users have an immersive feeling when they play the game.

Also, to make the game also interesting to play, we designed three obstacles to make users can experience new things in different levels.

  • Spawn proximity
    The higher the level, the greater the proximity where enemies can spawn
  • Teleport
    Monsters have 30% to teleport to the position with a same X coordinate when their HP reduces to 50%.
  • Shield
    A 2 seconds shield will be randomly activated on certain monsters that are close to the crosshair.

Once a level is done, the game show users the total points they collected based on the number of eliminated monsters. When users reach a certain amount of points, the game will unlock the next level with new monsters and rules. The calculation will be based on points collected for monsters eliminated and points deducted for monsters that touched the player. The monsters will spawn in 3 waves in every level.

And there are three levels in total with different difficulty. The game consists of 5 monsters and 2 special items, one being the rabbit which eliminates all monsters on your screen, and the other being the pad which players need to avoid shooting.

Process

Every design should have reason support it

After our team finished the game concept design, we delivered our ideas to Ant Hive, their game designers were responsible for all visual design of the VR game, such as moving, hitting, and killing status of each monster. Then I switched to handle the pre-game, game going, and post-game workflow design.

Below were requirements for my wireframe:

  1. The game feature should be easily noticed and the game can be quickly started.
  2. Creating a friendly experience for users to transfer from none VR part to VR part
  3. Adding a SOFY E-Commerce entry to boost products sales

To make the game feature outstanding, I added the new game entry button to the top of existed app and marked a “NEW” text to make it outstanding. After users click the button, they can get into the game. And during the loading time, I added a loading image to ease people’s tiring.

For the home page, I put the side navigation bar to leave enough space for the main content. The navigation bar contains 3 tabs：selecting levels, points redemption, and E-commerce. The default active tab is selecting levels. For selecting levels panel, it shows three levels which contain level’s title, level’s introduction, and functional buttons, such as tutorial button and start button. If the level is locked, I put a lock icon on the top of the panel.

After users click the tutorial button, I designed the slide tutorial which has two parts —about friend and about enemy. For each part, users can click the right bottom thumbnails to switch characters. Also, users have option to jump tutorial and I provided start button on the last panel. For the tutorial panel, the first time I overlooked many situations, which I will explain them later.

For the game flow, after users click the start button, the interface notifies users to put smartphone into glasses to play the game.

Because there is time-delay when users wear glasses, the game won’t start until users aim at the start button for 1 seconds. After one level finishes, users go to the game over page which presents all information of this round. And I also provided a progress bar to tell users how many points they still need to get to unlock next level.

On the redemption page, users can view their current points and redemption history. But due to the imitation of business issues, they can only redeem all points at once. I didn’t think it was a good marketing idea. I gave feedback to my director that I wanted users can directly user coupons to redeem SOFY’s products, which obviously produce more motivation for users to play the game and attract more people to use SOFY products. However, my director told me we could do nothing about this part.

Then I realized that design is not only about design a product, but also it relates to balance business goals and design goals.

Modification

Rapid iteration helps the team understand what the client really wants

After I collaborated with UI designer finishing the first high-fidelity version, I needed to prove my design towards client because the PM cannot speak Chinese with Chinese client. I was confident to deliver all my ideas to clients but then I realized that I missed many edge cases after clients pointed out their concerns.

Here were concerns clients had:

  1. The start game button was not conspicuous to see and button size should be bigger considering the VR scenario.
  2. The interaction mode of selecting levels was inconsistent. At the beginning users select levels by fingers, but in the end users select levels by eyes.
  3. The tutorial pages were tedious and we should let users browse them as quick as possible.
  4. The ending page lacked an sense of honor, which could motivate users to play longer.

After a deep consideration, I overthrew my previous design and came up with better ideas to solve these concerns. Firstly, I changed the layout of home page to highlight START button and enlarged button's size to make them easier to be aim by eyes.

Then I changed the way to select levels from manual control to eyes control, making it easier for users to switch levels without taking VR glasses off.

Also, I changed the browsing gesture of tutorial page from left & right clicking to up & down scrolling, which greatly expedited users' browsing speed.

Last but not least, I added extra details for the ending page that a pad and a rabbit fade in together to give users a sense of honor. Then characters fade out and level selection fades in.

After we put up the new design detailing all these changes, clients happily accepted our final design.

Reflection

Put yourself into users' shoes when you design

I realized that I made many design flaws because I designed the product mostly from my personal experience. I missed many edge cases from a real user’s point of view at the very beginning. I should have thought the whole game flow from an amateur gamer’s point of view. It’s necessary for designers to have the ability to switch to users’ thinking model.

For example, was the button a call to actions? CTA buttons must be adequately sized and a minimum of 44 points is usually sufficient. Was the button’s size big enough to click by using eye? it’s more difficult for users to click button by using head than fingers. Consider the usage scenarios is very important. What is the primary goal of users? After you decide the primary goal, try your best to make it stand out.

Also, clients are your users too. Design a smart way to collaborate with them and make them happy with your design.
