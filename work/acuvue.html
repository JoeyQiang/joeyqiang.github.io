Joey Qiang

Acuvue

  • UX Design
  • June, 2016
  • ACUVUE

ACUVUE was a WeChat-based Web APP that helps users get online support efficiently, such as Q&A, online shopping, and online redemption, which assist users to care for their eyes anytime anywhere, while enhancing Acuvue’s brand awareness and promoting its product sales.

Because ACUVUE wasn’t satisfied with their current APP, it asked MNG to give some improving suggestion. And as an intern UX designer at MNG, I was assigned the task of analyzing the current version and proposing a redesign solution so that the project manager could sell the solution to ACUVUE. It's my first project at MNG and I took it seriously because I felt excited to show my design abilities.

Problem

Defining existed problems took me forever

Since there were so many problems for the APP, after I went through all pages, I summarized its information architecture to help me better comb its logic.

Then, for each part of the APP, I tried to define its problems based on my knowledge about user experience design. If I had some questions, I would reach out for help from my director but I finished almost all of them. Here we went:

For the Instruction page, I thought instructions should be question-centered instead of answer-centered. And after I clicked one answer, the detail page would open up in a new web page, which forced me have to go back every time, but I preferred a smooth one-page reading experience.

For the Product page, the big images caused slow loading speed. Also, the huge annotation of “Clicking image to see details" wasn’t necessary since the card section should self-explain that it's clickable. Also, the top menu bar wasn’t conspicuous to be found by users.

For the Store finder page, the advertisement image not only didn’t increase user experience, but also hampered it, because it took up the hero area which made the core function, store location, hard to be found by users. Besides, when users searched a shop location, the useful map view feature was hidden in default. Instead, a text-based address showed up, which totally disrupted the order.

For the Free sample page, there were too many form fields to fill, which was crazy. And there was a minor technical bug that when you went into the page, the scroll bar always jumped down some distance, which distracted users’ browsing experience.

For the Coupon page, the heavy image introduction should be replaced with html and css, which could save network resource. Also, similar to free-sample page, users directly jumped down to the form area, completely skipping the coupon instruction area, which meant many users had to scroll back if they wanted to see instruction.

For the Points store page, there was no clue telling users how many points they already had. Also, if an unlogged user clicked a product to check details, there would be alert popping up telling the user to log in firstly. It would be better to directly jump to log in page when the website detected the unlogged status.

For the Gift redemption page, the redemption history button was only a text one, which didn’t look clickable. Also, the redemption process was redundant that asked users to do many unnecessary steps. Why wouldn’t it be more convenient for users? For example, a one-click redemption.

For the Redemption rule page, the title of “member card number” was meaningless for unauthenticated users but the title was always there. Also, the redemption rules should be simplified. Now it’s hard to for a ordinary person to figure them out.

For the Admin registration page, the page should not be here which could be viewed by users. It’s not a function for customers. And for the User registration page, firstly the registration form was also redundant. Secondly, the page title should be replaced with my account because the title of “user registration” wouldn’t make sense any more after users finished registration.

Process

User journey map helped me think of better solutions

Based on clients' information, I summarized three personas to help me understand different people’s requirements, each of them representing a certain type of people. Later, I put my self into each person’s position, imaging what would be a good user experience for myself. Here were the user journeys of three people:

  • First time user
    Amber is a first time user. She knows little about the contacts and needs to wear contacts for special occasions. She never use ACUVUE lenses or any other brand lenses.
    • Amber has no experience about purchasing ACCUVE products.
    • A clerk directs Amber to an optometrist to choose the contacts that fit her requirements and recommends the WeChat official account to Amber
    • Amber follows the official account then registers to get points and coupons. Now, she can use the points to redeem gifts, browse products and discover surrounding stores.
  • Experienced user
    John is an experienced user. He has been using ACUVUE contacts for a while but he never use ACUVUE WeChat account. He also needs to purchase contacts regularly.
    • John finds out he will get coupons if he registers as a member of ACUVUE on the WeChat account. He follows it and registers.
    • John buys the contacts with a coupon at the store. The clerk tells him that he accumulates the points that can be redeemed online.
    • John begins to accumulates points on purpose. Because he learns that he can order free samples online, he starts to pay attentions to promotion activities.
  • Loyal user
    Linda is loyal user. She wears ACUVUE contacUE conryday. She has been using ACUVUE WeChat account and is used to purchasing ACUVUE contacts online.
    • Linda runs out of her contacts and needs to buy more. She uses the Find stores function from the WeChat account to locate the nearest one.
    • Linda buys the contacts then aska a clerk how many points she can get. She asks a clerk if there are any new free samples she can try.
    • Linda uses points to redeem a gift. She pays attention to all new products and always be glad to order free samples, and uses coupons to buy products she likes.
Solution

Every design should have reason behind it

To start with, I simplified the information architecture based on the journey map.

For Product page, firstly I kept all three products within one page, saving users’ time to browse. Next, I changed the link of free sample to a more attractive CTA button which was put at the bottom of the page, motivating users to click it. Also, I classified coupons to limited and unlimited ones, motivating users to use coupon as soon as possible. In another words, motivating users to buy products as soon as possible.

For Guidance page, firstly I changed the layout to question-centered style. Then I added product recommendation banner to its related question, increasing the product’s selling conversion rate. Also, I changed the interaction method of viewing all answer from jumping to another page to expanding under the question.

For Store finder page, I directly used map view to give users a clear and straightforward searching experience. Specifically, I showed several surrounding stores in default based on user’s location. Besides, I put the nearest store's detailed information on the bottom of the page. Search function also be supported to satisfy users who wanted to search certain stores. After users selected the store, they could navigate to the location by using WeChat Map API.

For Coupon page, in default, users had two different types of coupons after they logged in, motivating them to purchase products. Also, the Back-end should send coupons to users from time to time, prompting users when they landed on the page. Another detail was that I provided a coupon link at the bottom of the page. Clients could use it to collect personal information from users if they wanted.

For Points & Redemption page, I redesigned the layout, giving a bottom link which could jump to redemption-rule page, which built the connection between two pages. Also, I added a progress bar to each product, informing users of remaining actions to exchange the product, which encouraged users to collect more points. After redeeming a product, users could view redemption history on My account page.

For My account page, besides changing the page title, I added coupons and redemption history to the page so users could know more information on the account page. Another minor detail was that the first time when users landed on the page, I asked them to sign up so they could directly use 100 points to redeem products, encouraging users to sign up.

Reflection

Focusing on details paid off in the end

This was a project relating much about improving user experience. It’s not a project which required you to draw a big vision and defined functions. However, in my opinion, I really enjoyed the experience, which you tried best to focus on each minor details and explored ways to improve them.

Though improving one or two details may not change much about a product, quantity will finally turn into quality. If you could make sure every detail in its best case, you could turn a good product into a perfect one. I believed that focusing on details, all your hard works would pay off in the end.
