Inplace

  • UX design
  • May, 2016
  • Service design course

INPLACE is a service exchange mobile APP that connects providers with those in need of services. This solution is designed to bring a shared sense of community to our seniors who want to share accumulated resources such as time, presence, and knowledge, with recipients looking for personal enrichment.

This was the final project of the class—Service Design, which I finished in January 2016. I collaborated with a teammate to finish all design, including research, problem identification, persona, storyboard, wireframe, Hi-Fi prototype and usability test. After I finished this project on a tight schedule, I realized that I really enjoy the design process and I’d love to take UX as my life career.

Context

It's time for us to think of ourselves 50 years from now

We discover from the below changing inverted pyramid that the United States is getting old. The decreasing number of children born in the country is changing how society operates and is impacting the country's aging population. In today’s world, elderly people sometimes are considered problems or burdens for their families and our society in general. Because of this, we need to think how today’s younger generation can improve the situation, not only for the current seniors, but also for themselves in the future.

Process

User-centered design is always my design principle

After discussion in class, we discovered that basic personal, household and transportation services were in high demand and in short supply for seniors. We decided to do research on ways to afford older adults greater autonomy, thus enabling them to live conveniently and longer in their own homes. After interviewing 6 seniors about what they needed, we summarized the figure below.

Then we built two personas from real examples to help us better understand our users:

  • Kelly 18 years old
    I am a senior student in high school who comes from a large family. While growing up, my parents shared with me the importance of volunteering and giving back. Therefore, when I moved off to college, I wanted to continue helping by maybe working in a hospital or nursing home. It will be nice to connect with others while saving a little money for school.
  • Mary 68 years old
    I am a retired nurse. I raised a family and have lived in this community for over 30 years. 4 months ago I experienced a mild stroke and it has become more difficult for me to take care of myself. The thought of moving to an assisted living home depresses me. I would like to stay in my home as long as I can and be around the things I love.

Then we began designing the user journey map, which built the general structure of our service design.

Based on the journey map, we drew a simplified skeleton of how our service APP worked and then we turned it into the interfaces.

Solution

When I thought it's fine, I pushed myself 5% more

To make users have a fantastic experience through the workflow, Harvey and I summarized 3 rules which we needed to follow when drawing the interfaces.

  1. Concision: It should be easy to use, and give users a sense of autonomy.
  2. Diversity: It allows users to search for various types of value or resources within the local community.
  3. Growth: It gives users the ability to rate their experience to better match them with future providers and support reciprocal exchanges.

Here were part of the details of the UI. After users lauched InPlace, they would see walkthrough-pages to get a general idea about the APP. Then they would be asked to answer several quesitons so that InPlace could later send customized content to them. There were four main parts of the APP.

First was the home page on which users could view posting information. On its top left, there was a identification switch button. Users could transfer their identification between the seniors and the youngsters by clicking the button. Accordingly, the home page content would change. On the top right, it's the posting button. After clicking it, users followed a straightforward four-step guidance to post a message.

The second part was the message part. Users could chat with other people on this page. Besides, they would receive system notifications on this page. The third part was the activity which seemed like a reminder calendar. After users accepted a task from another person, InPlace would automatically save the task's schedule on this page. The last part was the me part which contained users' personal information and history.
