2nd street facilitating a reciprocal group among Chinese students
2ND Street is a local craigslist-like classified advertisements site that helps Chinese students at KU to share useful information to each other, such as selling used products, renting hourse and interesting activities, ultimately facilitating students abroad to adapt to new environment more conviniently.

Project Background

Willing of helping my companions is always the courge & energy to push me keep the project going forward. If I can better other people’s life even a little bit, that would be my greatest comfort.

User Analysis

Who are the user

Who are the user

  • Main user are international Chinese students at KU who want to reduce their economic burden by purchasing used products. However, they lack a organized platform to post message
What should we do

What should we do

  • Make Chinese students become more familiar with our platform
  • Filter our user to be only students at KU for the sake of transaction security
  • Make user feel the price is super attractive
  • Make the platform to be as accessible as possible
  • Make the posting experience as friendly as possible
How do we do

How do we do

  • Use Chinese as website’s language to distinguish it from local English similar sites
  • Ask user to input their KU ID as an authentication
  • Ask user to input both original price and selling price
  • Build a mobile website that embedded in the matched offcial Wechat account
  • Put the posting function at a very high priority and greatly simplify the posting form

Reflection & Conclusion

  • Mobile first. Because mobile users has already become the mainstream, mobile version first would be rational for most cases.
  • Don’t allow remote working. Our team had a remote working developer for whom we always need to repeat everything again, which is inefficient.
  • Early stage teammates should share a same goal for the product. Without a same goal, team would nothave clear direction to forward together.
  • Don’t ignore the power of operation. Our team lack experienced people for operation, causing troubles when we want to expand market.
  • Business always needs money. Always set your business goal about how to make money by your product. Without exception, this project ends up with lacking money.
