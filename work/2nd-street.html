2nd street Facilitating a reciprocal group among Chinese students
2ND Street is a local craigslist-like classified advertisements site that helps Chinese students at KU to share useful information to each other, such as selling used products, renting house, and interesting activities, ultimately facilitating students abroad to adapt to new environment more easily.

Project Background

Willing to help people is always the power to encourage me to keep the project going forward. If I could better somebody’s life even a little bit, that would be my greatest comfort.

User Analysis

Who are the users

Who are the users

  • Main users is Chinese students at KU who want to reduce their economic burden by purchasing used products. However, they lack a local platform to post message
What should we do

What should we do

  • Make Chinese students become more familiar with our platform
  • Filter our users to be only students at KU for the sake of transaction security
  • Make users feel the price is super attractive
  • Make the platform to be as accessible as possible
  • Make the posting experience as friendly as possible
How do we do

How do we do

  • Use Chinese as website’s language to distinguish it from similar English sites
  • Ask users to input their KU ID as an authentication
  • Ask users to input both original price and selling price
  • Build a mobile website that embedded in the matched offcial Wechat account
  • Put the posting function at a very high priority and greatly simplify the posting form

Operation

CRM

  • Use QQ & Wechat group to maintain customer relationship
  • Post daily news in groups to keep users know updates
  • Create a closed loop among website, QQ and WeChat. Users can access to all of them by knowing anyone

Team Coordination

  • Use Worktile as the team collaboration platform
  • Launch new features and post bugs on the it
  • Upload project documents regularly to the it

CIM

  • Use DoraCMS as the commodity information management backend
  • Top attractive products and delete sold products

Product Marketing

  • Use WeChat Account backend as the product marketing tool
  • Send brand-related articles to users from time to time
  • Use its analytic module to analyze the data of users, articles and menus, to make a better decision

Conclusion

  • Mobile first. Because mobile users have become the mainstream users, creating a mobile version first would be rational in most cases.
  • Avoid remote working. My team had a remote working developer and sometimes he cannot collaborate with us effectively due to the time lag.
  • Early stage teammates should share the same goal for the product. Without the same goal, team wouldn't have a clear direction to forward together.
  • Value the power of operation. My team lack experienced people for operation, creating challenges when we want to expand market.
  • Business always needs money. Always set your business goal about how to make money by your product. This project ends up with lacking money.
